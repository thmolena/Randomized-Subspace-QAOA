Randomized Subspace QAOA

Residual-Controlled Active-Subspace Optimization for Multi-Angle QAOA

Molena Huynh

Preprint · 2026

Randomized Subspace QAOA turns the local observable sensitivity of multi-angle QAOA into a smaller optimization space. Matrix-free operator actions build the space, a randomized residual estimate tests whether it remains adequate, and recycled refreshes let the basis rotate as the optimizer moves. Released experiments show substantial parameter compression at depth one with a small paired quality change, and they expose both the quality loss and setup cost that appear at depth two.

01 / Problem

Why compress?

Multi-angle QAOA creates a growing search space

Standard depth-\(p\) QAOA shares two angles per layer. Multi-angle QAOA assigns one phase parameter to each cost term and one mixer parameter to each qubit, so the search dimension grows as \[ d=p\bigl(|E|+|V|\bigr). \] The additional coordinates help only when they produce independent changes in measurable observables.

For the per-edge expectation vector \(F(\theta)\), its Jacobian \(J(\theta)=\partial F/\partial\theta\) maps parameter perturbations to observable changes. For any edge-weight vector \(w\), \[ \nabla C(\theta)=J(\theta)^{\mathsf T}w, \] so the local objective gradient lies in the row space of \(J\). Because \(\operatorname{rank}(J)\leq\min(|E|,d)\), observable space supplies an algebraic compression ceiling even as depth enlarges the ambient parameter space.

03 / Method

Adaptive active subspaces

Build, optimize, and refresh without forming the Jacobian

RSQ never forms the full Jacobian. It uses Jacobian-vector and vector-Jacobian products to approximate \(A=J^{\mathsf T}\) by \(QB\), adding orthonormal blocks until a randomized residual estimate meets a requested tolerance or the algebraic rank ceiling is reached. The stopping status is recorded. Optimization then proceeds through \(\theta=\theta_0+Qz\), with \(z\) carrying only the retained active coordinates.

  1. 01

    Probe

    Apply matrix-free sensitivity actions to discover observable directions without materializing \(J\).

  2. 02

    Compress

    Factor the local map into an orthonormal parameter basis \(Q\) and optimize only its coordinates.

  3. 03

    Refresh

    Recheck the residual, recycle useful directions, and recompress when the local row space drifts.

Conditional local statement

What the true residual controls

The row space is local and can rotate during optimization. RSQ therefore rechecks the residual every 25 steps. A failed check triggers a recycled update that augments the old basis with current residual directions and recompresses the combined space. If the true residual obeys \(\|J(I-QQ^{\mathsf T})\|_2\leq\varepsilon\), then \[ \bigl|C(\theta+\Delta)-C(\theta+QQ^{\mathsf T}\Delta)\bigr| \leq \|w\|\,\varepsilon\,\|\Delta\|+L_C\|\Delta\|^2. \] The true residual controls local first-order sensitivity; its finite-probe estimate is a refresh diagnostic, not a global optimality certificate. The public result records every residual check and refresh, the rank trajectory, final and best-observed iterates, and separated operator counts.

04 / Boundaries

What the interfaces and probes establish

Forward-only access and an explicit confidence boundary

Forward-only path

No vector-Jacobian products

A distinct fixed-rank path removes every vector-Jacobian product, including those used during refresh. Central finite differences supply directional actions, \[ Jv=\frac{F(\theta_0+hv)-F(\theta_0-hv)}{2h}+O(h^2), \] Gaussian probes estimate average discarded sensitivity, and a two-evaluation simultaneous-perturbation estimate updates the reduced objective. A regression test enforces zero reverse products throughout basis construction, refresh, and optimization. The released results establish feasibility, not parity with two-sided RSQ.

Finite-probe envelope

A diagnostic, not a global certificate

For \(s\) Gaussian probes and failure probability \(\delta\), let \(t=2/\sqrt{s\delta}\). When \(t<1\), the true Frobenius residual ratio lies between \[ \sqrt{\frac{1-t}{1+t}}\,\widehat\rho_F \quad\text{and}\quad \sqrt{\frac{1+t}{1-t}}\,\widehat\rho_F \] with probability at least \(1-\delta\) under the exact linear-operator model. At the released \(s=12\) and 95% confidence, this elementary envelope is non-informative. The observed ratio is therefore a drift diagnostic; the local loss theorem remains conditional on the true residual.

05 / Evidence

Released CPU benchmark

Compression, quality, and measured operator cost

The released CPU study contains 36 paired MaxCut runs on 14 unique topologies (28 topology-depth clusters) across three graph families, \(n\in\{8,10\}\), depths \(p\in\{1,2\}\), and three restart identifiers. Full, fixed-rank, two-sided, and forward-only methods share the same initial angle vector; the symmetry baseline starts at its orbit-wise projection. Graph, initialization, and sketch seeds are separate, and every row stores a canonical edge list and graph hash. Step budgets are matched, and operator costs are counted.

Trace every value. The cards below summarize the committed artifact. Audit the row-level CSV, machine-readable summary, and paper protocol together.

Depth one

Compression with a small paired quality change

Full multi-angle QAOA optimizes 23.7 parameters on average and attains approximation ratio \(0.862\pm0.053\). Two-sided RSQ at tolerance \(10^{-2}\) optimizes 14.7 parameters, a 39.1% mean cluster-level reduction, and attains \(0.867\pm0.051\). The paired change is \(+0.48\pm0.94\) percentage points, reported as mean plus or minus two standard errors across 14 topology clusters. Forward-only rank-8 RSQ compresses by 64.7% and changes quality by \(-6.39\pm1.98\) points.

Depth two

A measured failure boundary

At depth two, the average ambient dimension grows to 47.4 while RSQ retains 14.7 directions, a 69.6% reduction. The paired quality change becomes \(-4.77\pm2.47\) percentage points. The loss occurs across the tested families and rules out a blanket quality claim. Forward-only rank-8 RSQ compresses by 82.3% with a \(-14.73\pm2.65\)-point change.

Cost accounting

Measured operator cost, without a query-advantage claim

Instrumented counters separate forward evaluations, Jacobian-vector products, and vector-Jacobian products. On these small exact simulators, subspace construction and refresh require more operator applications than direct full-space optimization. At the primary tolerance, two-sided RSQ averages 749/134/481 forward/JVP/VJP actions at depth one and 867/155/556 at depth two. The categories overlap and are not physical-shot totals. The supported result is controlled parameter compression, not query advantage. All 324 of 324 scheduled checks triggered rebuilding, so this grid establishes no empirical saving from the gate. For this fixed weighted objective, one VJP returns the scalar gradient directly; multi-weight amortization is untested. Full-space SPSA also costs two objective evaluations per step, and no such baseline is included for the forward-only comparison.

Selected rank

Residual-controlled active dimension

Tightening the tested tolerance from \(10^{-1}\) to \(10^{-2}\) leaves mean rank unchanged while quality differs modestly: RSQ retains 14.7 directions on average, approximately the observable count. The residual identifies the algebraic ceiling rather than additional singular-value decay, while the ambient dimension doubles with depth. Five of 144 builds at tolerance \(10^{-2}\) and three of 144 at \(10^{-1}\) reached the ceiling before the sampled indicator met its requested tolerance.

06 / Package

Install, inspect, extend

A reusable numerical core with an auditable research artifact

The repository root and code/ directory build the same rsqaoa project. The installable distribution contains the statevector simulator, backend-neutral sensitivity adapter, randomized factorization, optimizers, baselines, and console command. Experiment scripts, released results, paper sources, and generated figures remain checkout-only research artifacts rather than hidden wheel payloads.

Install from Git now

python -m pip install \
  "rsqaoa @ git+https://github.com/thmolena/Randomized-Subspace-QAOA.git#subdirectory=code"

rsqaoa-experiment --family ring --n 4 --p 1 --steps 1 --json

For a frozen environment, replace the default branch with a release tag or commit SHA. Bare pip install rsqaoa requires a future, separately authenticated package-index release and is not claimed here.
