\section*{Popular Summary}

Quantum optimization experiments can become expensive when every new objective
requires a fresh search over many circuit parameters.  We investigate whether
related optimization tasks on the same graph can share a smaller set of useful
parameter directions.  The method identifies those directions from how the
circuit's observable outputs respond to parameter changes and refreshes them
when a residual diagnostic indicates drift.  On the tested small
exact-statevector benchmarks, the reduced representation captures meaningful
local structure, but it does not deliver an operational saving: the
residual-gated method misses its declared quality threshold and uses more
forward circuit evaluations than full-space optimization.  A finite-shot
objective pilot also retains simulator-only derivatives.  The main outcome is
therefore a transparent mathematical construction and resource ledger,
together with a reproducible negative result that identifies what future
hardware-scale tests must change.
