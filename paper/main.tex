\begin{abstract}
Repeated optimization tasks on one graph can differ only in observable
weights, suggesting reuse of circuit sensitivity geometry.  We define a
task-distribution-weighted observable tangent space for multi-angle QAOA.  If
$\M=\E[ww^{\trans}]=LL^{\trans}$, leading left singular vectors of
$\J^{\trans}L$ minimize expected discarded local gradient energy.  We construct
it matrix-free from a training bank and evaluate independent weight
trajectories with matched full- and reduced-space SPSA.  Across 16
exact-statevector topology--depth cells from eight graphs, residual-gated reuse
changes mean approximation ratio by $-1.57\pm1.04$ points (descriptive mean
$\pm2$ s.e.) and requires $1.218\times$ the forward evaluations of full SPSA,
failing the gate.  Its 10.05-point advantage over a
rank-8 random basis is not refresh- or cost-matched.  In a separate 256-shot
objective pilot on eight graphs, per-task refresh gives $+0.61\pm1.57$ points,
but construction uses exact observables and simulator VJPs.  Neither
demonstrates query, hardware or quantum advantage.  The study defines a
falsifiable representation and the evidence required.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA) alternates problem and
mixer unitaries and tunes their angles with a classical optimizer
\citep{farhi2014qaoa,hadfield2019quantum}.  Multi-angle QAOA (ma-QAOA) replaces
the two shared angles per layer with one phase angle per cost term and one
mixer angle per vertex \citep{herrman2022multiangle}.  For a graph with
$m=|E|$ edges, $n=|V|$ vertices and depth $p$, this raises the search dimension
from $2p$ to
\begin{equation}
 d=p(m+n).
 \label{eq:dimension}
\end{equation}
The additional coordinates can improve expressivity, but only their action on
measurable observables can affect an objective.  This creates a natural
question: can the locally relevant parameter directions be learned once and
reused?

The question becomes concrete when one graph supports a sequence of related
objectives.  Let $F(\bm\theta)\in\R^m$ contain the expected edge-cut
observables and let a task be the scalarization
$C_w(\bm\theta)=w^{\trans}F(\bm\theta)$.  Such weight families arise when
priorities, scenarios or Pareto scalarizations change while the circuit
architecture remains fixed.  Multi-objective QAOA makes this repeated-task
setting operationally relevant \citep{kotil2025multiobjective}.  At any fixed
anchor, all task gradients satisfy
$\nabla C_w=\J^{\trans}w$, where
$\J=\partial F/\partial\bm\theta$.  A representation of the range of
$\J^{\trans}$ is therefore reusable across weights.

That algebra alone is insufficient.  For a single scalar objective, one
vector-Jacobian product already returns its complete gradient, while SPSA uses
two scalar objective evaluations per iteration in either full or reduced
coordinates \citep{spall1992spsa}.  Constructing a basis can consequently cost
more than direct optimization.  A useful study must specify the task
distribution, prevent future evaluation weights from entering construction,
compare matched optimizers, and report basis, refresh and measurement costs
without converting simulator differentiation into fictitious hardware
queries.

We develop and audit such a protocol.  The representation is weighted by the
second moment of a training task distribution, approximated with randomized QB
through products with $\J^{\trans}L$ and its transpose.  An independent
evaluation trajectory tests transfer.  Full-space and reduced-space SPSA use
the same 40-step schedule and the same number of scalar objective calls except
for an explicitly counted residual gate.  Six controls probe reusable geometry,
refresh timing and construction cost without isolating all three at matched
total cost.  We define a development
criterion before interpreting the pilot: the gated method must be no more than
one approximation-ratio point below full SPSA, exceed a rank-8 random basis
with the same maximum rank cap, and use at most $1.25\times$ the full method's
forward evaluations.

The outcome is deliberately not rewritten into a success claim.  The learned
subspace exceeds a random subspace in an unmatched comparison, but the exact gated method
misses the quality condition and refreshes on most opportunities.  A small
finite-shot pilot is directionally favorable for per-task refresh but is
not end-to-end sampled and is statistically unresolved.  The contribution is
therefore twofold: a theorem and implementation for task-weighted tangent
representations, and a matched audit that establishes where the present
workflow fails to become an operational advantage.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}
\item We prove that the leading left singular subspace of
$\J^{\trans}L$ is the optimal rank-$r$ parameter representation for expected
squared local gradient retention under a specified task-weight second moment.
\item We implement the operator without materializing a dense Jacobian and
separate basis-training weights from a disjoint evaluation trajectory that
shares only the fixed generative weight model.
\item We conduct a matched full-versus-reduced SPSA audit with no-refresh,
fixed-refresh, residual-gated, per-task, random-refresh and random-basis
controls, and an additive ledger for scalar objectives, vector observables,
shots, sensitivity actions, subspace builds, checks and refreshes.
\item We report the failed exact development gate and the inconclusive hybrid
finite-shot pilot as observed, then state a concrete confirmatory and hardware
path rather than claiming query or quantum advantage.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Related work and scope}

Graph symmetries can tie ma-QAOA angles before optimization
\citep{shi2022notallangles}.  Active-subspace analysis instead uses averaged
gradient outer products to find influential coordinates
\citep{constantine2014active}.  Randomized range finding and fixed-precision QB
provide matrix-free tools for approximating such spaces
\citep{halko2011finding,yu2018randqb,martinsson2020randomized}.  Noise-aware
random-subspace optimizers address a different object: they adapt a classical
search subspace and local model under noisy scalar observations rather than
factor a vector-observable sensitivity operator
\citep{dzahini2025anastaars}.  Broad optimizer comparisons also caution that
dimension and evaluation count do not rank variational methods by themselves
\citep{bonetmonroig2023optimizers}.

A graph-conditioned trust-region policy in the first preprint version (now
superseded) transfers a pre-query search strategy across graph instances for
standard $2p$-angle QAOA
\citep{huynh2026gctr}; the present method is instead within-instance and seeks
to reuse the $p(m+n)$-parameter ma-QAOA observable tangent geometry across a
family of objective weights.  This distinction is the purpose of the
cross-reference and is not evidence for the present method.

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{A task-weighted reusable tangent representation}

For an orthonormal basis $Q\in\R^{d\times r}$ and projector
$P=QQ^{\trans}$, the task-averaged gradient energy lost outside the basis is
\begin{equation}
 \E_w\norm{(\I-P)\nabla C_w(\bm\theta)}_2^2
 =\norm{(\I-P)\J(\bm\theta)^{\trans}L}_F^2,
 \qquad \M=\E[ww^{\trans}]=LL^{\trans}.
 \label{eq:taskloss}
\end{equation}
Theorem~\ref{thm:taskweighted} shows that the leading left singular vectors of
$\J^{\trans}L$ minimize this quantity.  This is stronger than using the
unweighted row space when some observable combinations are common and others
are irrelevant to the task distribution.  It is also explicitly local: the
basis can rotate as $\bm\theta$ changes.

We estimate $\M$ from eight training weight vectors.  Training and evaluation
trajectories use independent random seeds and no evaluation weight enters the
factor used by randomized QB.  They share a fixed per-graph low-rank weight
model, which is exactly the assumed transfer relation.  Figure~\ref{fig:protocol}
shows the separation and the falsification-aware audit.

\subsection{The exact development gate fails under a matched SPSA audit}

The exact-statevector pilot contains eight distinct graph topologies from
random 3-regular and Erdos--Renyi families, $n\in\{8,10\}$.  Evaluating each
topology at $p\in\{1,2\}$ gives 16 configured topology--depth analysis cells;
the two depths reuse each topology.  Every cell contains eight evaluation
tasks, and each method receives 40 SPSA steps per
task.  Full and reduced SPSA therefore each use 648 scalar objective
evaluations over a stream, except that the residual-gated method uses 28
additional scalar evaluations for seven one-probe checks.

Full SPSA obtains mean approximation ratio $0.8244\pm0.0230$, where uncertainty
throughout this section is a descriptive two-standard-error summary over those
16 cells, without modeling their cross-depth dependence.
Residual-gated reuse obtains $0.8087\pm0.0237$.  Its paired difference is
$-1.57\pm1.04$ percentage points.  The method therefore misses the
prespecified no-worse-than-one-point condition, although it satisfies the
forward-cost ceiling and the random-basis comparison.  The composite
development gate is false.

Per-task recycled refresh is the strongest reduced method in the exact pilot, with
mean ratio $0.8136\pm0.0262$ and paired difference
$-1.08\pm0.94$ points.  This is close to, but still below, the one-point
development margin.  Because the margin and method were selected during
development, neither result is a confirmatory equivalence test.

\subsection{Controls are consistent with useful geometry, not operational advantage}

The rank-8 random basis loses $11.62\pm1.69$ points relative to full SPSA.
Residual-gated reuse exceeds that control by 10.05 points on average.  This gap
is consistent with useful task-weighted geometry, but the random-basis control
is not matched for refresh or construction cost and cannot isolate a causal
geometric contribution.  A basis built
once with no refresh loses $10.45\pm2.16$ points, which is consistent with loss
of local-space validity over the warm-started stream.  Fixed refresh every
two tasks loses $4.07\pm1.34$ points, and a Bernoulli random-refresh schedule
loses $5.63\pm2.17$ points.  These lower-refresh controls also differ in cost,
so they do not isolate refresh timing at a fixed budget.

The residual gate does not demonstrate selective savings.  It checks each of
the seven task transitions and triggers on 6.25 transitions on average, or
89.3\% of opportunities.  Per-task refresh updates the recycled basis on all
seven transitions.  The
gated method reduces the mean optimized dimension from 36.19 to 6.61, but uses
676 scalar-objective and 113.25 vector-observable forward evaluations, compared
with 648 and zero for full SPSA.  Its total forward count is 789.25, or
$1.218\times$ full SPSA, and construction uses a further 138.88 simulator-only
VJPs.  Per-task refresh uses 776 forward evaluations and 153.50 VJPs.  These
counts establish compression and a useful geometric control; they do not
establish fewer queries, lower wall time or hardware deployability.

Figure~\ref{fig:stream} shows that all structured methods improve as one task
warm-starts the next, but the random basis remains far below the learned spaces.
The gated refresh rate remains high across the stream.  A useful gate would
need to predict when refreshing changes downstream performance, not merely
produce a large finite-difference residual.

\subsection{The finite-shot signal is unresolved and hybrid}

We conduct a separate $p=2$ comparison with 256 computational-basis samples for
each scalar objective evaluation, no readout error, and three measurement seeds.
The seeds are nested repeats within each of eight graph topologies at $p=2$ and are
averaged before uncertainty is calculated; they are not treated as 24
independent graphs.  Full SPSA obtains mean ratio $0.8387\pm0.0376$.  The
residual-gated method obtains $0.8353\pm0.0392$, a paired difference of
$-0.33\pm1.67$ points.  Per-task refresh obtains $0.8448\pm0.0345$, a paired
difference of $+0.61\pm1.57$ points.  The descriptive uncertainty is too large
to motivate a directional claim; no formal directional or equivalence test was
performed.

This separate configuration is not a controlled noise ablation of the exact
$p=2$ cells.  Initialization and optimizer seeds include the configuration's
job index; removing the $p=1$ jobs changes that index for later cells.  The
exact and shot summaries must therefore not be differenced as if they shared
identical latent optimizer trajectories.

The pilot is not an end-to-end finite-shot subspace method.  Shot sampling is
used for SPSA objectives and gated residual calls, whereas every subspace build
uses exact-statevector observable evaluations, finite-difference JVPs and
reverse-mode VJPs.  Full SPSA and per-task refresh each use 165,888 scalar
objective shots, but per-task refresh additionally uses 125 vector-observable
parameter points and 150.92 simulator-only VJPs on average.  Gating uses 173,056
objective shots because its residual checks add 28 scalar calls, plus 95.67
exact vector-observable points and 120.42 simulator-only VJPs.  The recorded
shot counts are therefore only the scalar-objective component of a hybrid
simulator protocol, not a total hardware shot cost.

\section{Discussion}

The theorem gives a positive structural conclusion: for a specified weight
second moment, its leading tangent directions minimize discarded local
gradient energy.  Empirically, the learned subspace exceeds a random basis with
the same maximum rank cap, but that control is not refresh- or cost-matched and hence does
not causally isolate the geometry.  The representation also reduces the number
of optimized coordinates by more than four fifths on this grid.  Neither
statement is an operational advantage by itself.

The matched audit rejects the stronger development hypothesis.  Full SPSA has
the same two-evaluation-per-step property as reduced SPSA, and it avoids basis
construction.  Gating adds objective calls and suppresses few rebuilds.  The
exact quality deficit exceeds the prespecified one-point margin.  Per-task
refresh comes closest to the full baseline, which suggests that local-space
rotation, rather than randomized QB accuracy alone, is the main obstacle.  It
also incurs the largest reverse-mode burden.

The finite-shot pilot does not reverse this conclusion.  Its per-task mean is
directionally above full SPSA, but eight graph topologies do not resolve the
effect and the basis is still supplied by exact simulator sensitivities.  In
particular, equal scalar-objective shots do not imply equal total quantum cost.
All commuting MaxCut edge observables can be estimated from common bitstrings,
but the number of shots required for a vector-observable JVP depends on the
precision target, noise, compilation and estimator design.  Assigning a
hardware conversion to an automatic-differentiation VJP would fabricate a cost
model, so we leave it explicitly unconverted.

A decisive next study should be locked before new outcomes are inspected.  A
concrete confirmatory and hardware path is:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Freeze the estimand.}  Use the paired difference in mean
approximation ratio across topology--depth units, with a one-point equivalence
margin.  Treat total physical shots and QPU time as co-primary cost outcomes.
The observed exact paired standard deviation for gated reuse is 2.08 points;
under a Gaussian zero-effect planning approximation, about 35 independent
units provide 80\% power against a one-point margin.  A final power analysis
should add attrition, family strata and multiplicity before locking the sample.
\item \textbf{Test amortization directly.}  Evaluate task-bank sizes
$K\in\{1,2,4,8,16,32\}$ with training and evaluation banks generated or
collected independently.  Report the break-even task count using all
construction, refresh and optimization resources.
\item \textbf{Make construction hardware-native.}  Replace simulator VJPs with
a specified forward-only or parameter-shift estimator; allocate shots to every
scalar and vector-observable circuit point; and record compiled two-qubit gates,
circuit batches, queue time and wall time.  The current hybrid pilot is not a
substitute for this step.
\item \textbf{Retain matched controls.}  Compare full-space SPSA, no refresh,
fixed refresh, gated refresh, random refresh, per-task recycled refresh and a
rank-8 random basis under the same maximum rank cap at matched optimizer and
total-resource budgets.  Add a
gate-oracle analysis that asks whether each refresh improves the subsequent
task, then calibrate the residual against that event.
\item \textbf{Stress the assumed task model.}  Include unseen graph families,
larger $n$ and $p$, full-rank or changepoint weight streams, device noise and at
least one real backend.  Report failures by stratum rather than averaging them
away.
\end{enumerate}
Passing this protocol would support an amortized hardware-efficiency claim.
The present experiments do not.

\section{Methods}

\subsection{Multi-angle QAOA and vector observables}

For an undirected graph $G=(V,E)$ and edge $e=(i,j)$, define
$C_e=(I-Z_iZ_j)/2$.  A depth-$p$ ma-QAOA state is
\begin{equation}
 |\psi(\bm\theta)\rangle=
 \prod_{\ell=1}^{p}
 \left[\prod_{i\in V}e^{-i\beta_{\ell i}X_i}
 \prod_{e\in E}e^{-i\gamma_{\ell e}C_e}\right]
 |+\rangle^{\otimes n}.
 \label{eq:state}
\end{equation}
The parameter vector contains $p(m+n)$ real angles.  The observable map and its
Jacobian are
\begin{equation}
 F(\bm\theta)=\big(\langle\psi(\bm\theta)|C_e|\psi(\bm\theta)\rangle\big)_{e\in E},
 \qquad
 \J(\bm\theta)=\frac{\partial F}{\partial\bm\theta}\in\R^{m\times d}.
 \label{eq:jacobian}
\end{equation}
For positive edge weights $w$, the objective is
$C_w(\bm\theta)=w^{\trans}F(\bm\theta)$ and
$\nabla C_w=\J^{\trans}w$ by the chain rule.

\subsection{Task-weighted optimality theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Optimal task-weighted local parameter subspace]
\label{thm:taskweighted}
Let $w\in\R^m$ be square-integrable with second moment
$\M=\E[ww^{\trans}]=LL^{\trans}$.  Fix a differentiable anchor
$\bm\theta$ with Jacobian $\J\in\R^{m\times d}$ and set
$A=\J^{\trans}L$.  For any $Q\in\R^{d\times r}$ with
$Q^{\trans}Q=I$ and $P=QQ^{\trans}$,
\begin{equation}
 \E\norm{(\I-P)\nabla C_w(\bm\theta)}_2^2
 =\norm{(\I-P)A}_F^2.
 \label{eq:identity}
\end{equation}
If $A=U\Sigma V^{\trans}$, the projector
$P_r=U_rU_r^{\trans}$ onto its leading $r$ left singular vectors minimizes
Eq.~\eqref{eq:identity} over all rank-$r$ orthogonal projectors, with minimum
$\sum_{j>r}\sigma_j(A)^2$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Because $\nabla C_w=\J^{\trans}w$ and $\I-P$ is symmetric and idempotent,
\begin{align*}
 \E\norm{(\I-P)\J^{\trans}w}_2^2
 &=\operatorname{tr}\!\left[(\I-P)\J^{\trans}\M\J(\I-P)\right]\\
 &=\operatorname{tr}\!\left[(\I-P)AA^{\trans}(\I-P)\right]
 =\norm{(\I-P)A}_F^2.
\end{align*}
The Eckart--Young--Mirsky theorem, or equivalently the Ky Fan maximum
principle applied to $\operatorname{tr}(PAA^{\trans})$, gives the optimal
rank-$r$ projector and the discarded singular-value sum.
\end{proof}

For a training bank $\W\in\R^{K\times m}$ whose rows are task weights, the
implementation uses
\begin{equation}
 \widehat\M=\frac{1}{K}\W^{\trans}\W=LL^{\trans},
 \qquad L=\frac{1}{\sqrt K}\W^{\trans}.
 \label{eq:empiricalmoment}
\end{equation}
The theorem then holds exactly for the empirical training distribution.  It
does not guarantee transfer to a different evaluation distribution, global
optimization convergence, or preservation after the anchor moves.

\subsection{Matrix-free construction and objective-conditioned refresh}

Randomized QB accesses $A=\J^{\trans}L$ through
\begin{equation}
 A v=\J^{\trans}(Lv),
 \qquad
 A^{\trans}u=L^{\trans}(\J u),
 \label{eq:actions}
\end{equation}
using a simulator VJP for the first action and a central-finite-difference JVP
for the second.  The development configuration uses maximum rank 8, block size
2, relative tolerance 0.10, six norm probes and four residual probes.  A build
can stop at tolerance or at the available rank; its status, convergence flag
and residual are persisted in the result CSV.  A refresh seeds QB with the
current basis but permits the resulting span to
rotate.

For task $w_t$ at current $\bm\theta$, the gate estimates the relative omitted
objective-gradient energy.  For Gaussian direction $v$ it computes central
differences along $v$ and $(\I-P)v$.  With $s$ probes,
\begin{equation}
 \widehat\rho^2=
 \frac{\sum_{j=1}^{s}
 [D_{(\I-P)v_j}C_{w_t}(\bm\theta)]^2}
 {\sum_{j=1}^{s}[D_{v_j}C_{w_t}(\bm\theta)]^2}.
 \label{eq:gate}
\end{equation}
Each probe requires four scalar objective evaluations.  Its numerator and
denominator separately estimate squared gradient norms under exact directional
derivatives, but their ratio is not unbiased and one probe is not a calibrated
confidence bound.  The exact pilot uses $s=1$, finite-difference step 0.01 and
refresh threshold 0.30.  The finite-shot pilot uses step 0.10 to reduce noise
amplification.  The gate is therefore an operational heuristic, not a theorem.

The refresh controls are: one initial build only (no refresh); rebuild at tasks
2, 4 and 6 (fixed); check and rebuild above threshold at tasks 1--7 (gated);
refresh a recycled basis for every task (per-task); independent refresh
probability 0.35 at each
transition (random); and one isotropic Gaussian orthonormal basis of maximum
rank 8 with no sensitivity construction (random basis).

\subsection{Matched full and reduced SPSA}

For either native parameters or reduced coordinates $z$ with
$\bm\theta=\bm\theta_0+Qz$, SPSA uses a Rademacher vector $\delta_k$ and
\begin{equation}
 \widehat g_k=
 \frac{C( x_k+c_k\delta_k)-C(x_k-c_k\delta_k)}{2c_k}\,\delta_k,
 \label{eq:spsa}
\end{equation}
where $x_k$ denotes $\bm\theta_k$ or $z_k$.  We use 40 steps,
$a_k=0.12/(k+1+5)^{0.602}$,
$c_k=0.10/(k+1)^{0.101}$, one perturbation average, and maximum update norm
0.40.  One final evaluation gives $2\times40+1=81$ scalar objective calls per
task and 648 per eight-task stream.  The highest observed candidate becomes
the warm start for the next task.  Full and reduced methods share the same
initial angle and seed schedule, although their perturbation vectors live in
different dimensions.

\subsection{Weight streams and graph design}

For each graph, a seeded model draws a positive base vector and an orthonormal
loading matrix of latent rank 3.  A latent AR(1) process with persistence 0.85
and innovation scale 0.18 perturbs the weights through those loadings.  Values
are clipped below at 0.10 and each task vector is normalized to mean one, so
objective scale cannot masquerade as optimizer improvement.  The training and
evaluation streams use different seeds but the same base and loading model.
This is a controlled synthetic transfer setting, not a claim about naturally
occurring task distributions.

The exact grid contains random 3-regular and Erdos--Renyi graphs with edge
probability 0.5, $n\in\{8,10\}$, graph seeds 41 and 42, and
$p\in\{1,2\}$.  It contains eight graph topologies and 16 configured
topology--depth analysis cells; depths reuse topology.  The finite-shot grid
retains $p=2$, hence eight graph topologies and eight cells.  Brute-force weighted
MaxCut supplies the denominator of each approximation ratio.  A complex-128
PyTorch statevector backend supplies the exact observable map
\citep{paszke2019pytorch}.

\subsection{Exact and shot evaluators}

The exact evaluator returns statevector expectations.  The shot evaluator
samples computational-basis bitstrings from the same statevector and computes
all commuting edge observables from each bitstring.  The pilot uses 256 shots
per scalar objective call and zero readout error.  Measurement and SPSA seeds
are separate.  Candidate selection is based on the sampled objective, but the
reported primary approximation ratio is the exact statevector expectation at
the selected candidate.  This post-evaluation makes optimizer comparisons
less noisy but further distinguishes the pilot from a hardware-only workflow.

Subspace construction does not call the shot evaluator.  It uses exact
observable-map calls, finite-difference JVPs and automatic-differentiation
VJPs.  Thus ``256-shot pilot'' describes its scalar objective path only.

\subsection{Resource ledger}

Every task emits cumulative and task-local counts for:
\begin{itemize}
\item scalar objective parameter points;
\item vector-observable parameter points;
\item computational-basis shots used by the evaluator;
\item sensitivity JVPs and simulator-only VJPs;
\item subspace builds, residual checks and triggered refreshes.
\end{itemize}
Scalar-objective and vector-observable points are non-overlapping, and their sum
is reported as forward circuit evaluations.  JVP is an operation-level counter
whose underlying finite-difference points are already represented by
vector-observable evaluations; it is not added again.  A VJP is recorded in a
separate simulator-only category because no physical circuit conversion is
assumed.  Builds, checks and refreshes are events rather than circuit costs.
This ledger prevents double counting while preserving implementation detail.

\subsection{Analysis and development criterion}

For each method and topology--depth cell we average approximation ratio across
the eight sequential tasks.  In the finite-shot pilot, the three measurement
repeats are first averaged within the same cell.  Paired differences subtract
full SPSA in the same cell.  We report means and $2s/\sqrt N$, where $s$ is the
sample standard deviation of cell summaries.  In the exact pilot, the 16 cells
reuse eight topologies across two depths and their dependence is not modeled.
The resulting two-standard-error bars are descriptive, not formal confidence
intervals, independent-sample uncertainty estimates or equivalence tests.

The development-only gate requires residual-gated reuse to (i) have paired
mean difference at least $-0.01$ relative to full SPSA, (ii) exceed a random
basis with the same maximum rank cap, and (iii) use at most $1.25\times$ the
mean forward circuit evaluations of full SPSA.  Because hyperparameters and this criterion
were examined on the development grid, a fresh locked dataset is required for
confirmatory inference.

\section{Limitations}

The exact pilot has eight graph topologies, two small sizes, depths one and two,
two graph families and a synthetic rank-3 task model.  It is a development
grid, not a locked test.  Its descriptive error bars use 16 configured
topology--depth cells without modeling the reuse of each topology across depth.
The task second moment is estimated from only eight training weights.  Each
topology--depth cell has one seeded training bank and one evaluation trajectory;
shot repeats vary measurements, not task streams.  Generalization over stream
realizations is therefore not separately identified from graph variation.
Hyperparameters are not independently tuned for each control.  Warm-started
tasks are dependent within a stream.

The residual estimate uses one Gaussian probe and is not calibrated.  The
randomized-QB stop status and residual are persisted, but its four-probe
residual indicator is randomized rather than a confidence certificate.  The
randomized basis has maximum rank 8, and quality may change under larger ranks,
different observable dictionaries or dense-oracle construction.  The theorem
is local and makes no statement about SPSA convergence, finite-difference bias
or basis rotation.  The exact simulator stops at $n=10$ and does not test
large-scale statevector, tensor-network or QPU execution.

The finite-shot pilot has eight graph topologies and no device noise.  Its
configuration-dependent job indices change initialization and optimizer seeds
relative to the exact grid, so it is not a controlled noise comparison.  Only
scalar objective calls are sampled; subspace construction and primary
post-evaluation remain exact-statevector operations.  Its shot count is
therefore incomplete as a hardware ledger.  No claim is made about quantum
advantage, QPU speed, wall-clock reduction, asymptotic scaling, Pareto-front
quality or deployment.

\section{Conclusion}

Task-distribution-weighted observable tangent spaces give a precise local
answer to which ma-QAOA directions matter across a family of scalarized
objectives.  The matched audit gives an equally precise empirical boundary.
On the present exact grid, the learned subspace exceeds a random basis in an
unmatched comparison but remains below full SPSA and costs more forward
evaluations; the residual
gate rarely avoids refreshing.  The directionally positive finite-shot
per-task result is unresolved and depends on exact simulator sensitivities.
The correct conclusion is therefore not query efficiency, but a reusable
representation whose operational case remains unproven.  The released controls,
ledger and confirmatory path make that case falsifiable.
